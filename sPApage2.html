Kansas Property Tax Appraisals

softPetroleum Appraiser™ (sPA) provides specialized tools for calculating property tax value of oil and gas properties using the various ad-valorem tax appraisal methods practiced in states that make an individual determination of the market value of each petroleum producing property every year. Valuation using cash flow or market approaches to valuation are practiced in a number of states including Kansas, Texas, Utah, and California. Taxpayers and appraisers/assessors in states that individually determine lease property values are the primary users that the features and functionality of softPetroleum Appraiser have been optimized for. In this quick tour, we will introduce the property tax appraisal features optimized for Kansas.

softPetroleum Appraiser™ provides comprehensive, easy to use tools for valuing (and rendering) Kansas properties using the mass appraisal method proscribed by the Kansas Department of Revenue’s Oil and Gas Appraisal Guide (the “Guide method”).

The system provides easy generation of the Kansas Oil & Gas Renditions, together with all supporting documents (such as division orders, expense documentation, rate-time decline curves, itemized equipment lists, etc.) which may be required to support the filed renditions.

Alternative valuation methods used in Kansas and supported by softPetroleum Appraiser™ include a "modified KS Guide" valuation approach which uses longer term determinations of the production decline rate within the overall framework of the KS Guide calculation method. Additional Guide method modifications including the taking an excess expense allowance which can be automatically considered and selectively applied when beneficial if actual expense data is imported into the data layer attached to softPetroleum Appraiser™.

softPetroleum Appraiser™ fully supports additional market valuation approaches widely used in the broader oil and gas industry. sPA includes comprehensive discounted cash flow analysis (DCF) tools which can provide useful alternative valuations similar to approaches used in most other states. DCF is also the primary valuation methodology used in petroleum property acquisition/sales.

Other alternative approaches to the Kansas Oil and Gas Appraisal Guide proscribed value have been used over the years and softPetroleum Appraiser™ fully supports each alternate approach. For example, calculations based on the determination of in-place reserves using decline curve analysis or gas material balance combined with an actual valuation based on comparative reserves sales were introduced several years ago and are still supported by the software. softPetroleum Appraiser™ will always seamlessly integrate automatic comparison of valuation results for all accepted methods of valuation for petroleum assets.
